Mukimo Recipe

Description

Mukimo is a popular Kenyan one-pot dish consisting of potatoes, corn, beans, peas, and greens that are cooked in water with some salt, then mashed into a pur√©e. The name of the dish is derived from the word kima, meaning to mash.

It is traditionally served at Kenyan celebrations, especially funerals and weddings, either as it is, or as an accompaniment to chicken or beef stews.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of maize off the cob
  • 2 tbs salt
  • 2 cups of green peas
  • 6 medium-sized potatoes
  • 50g butter or margarine
  • A bunch of Pumpkin leaves (kahurura)

Steps

  1. The first step is to prepare the kahurura or pumpkin leaves, ensure they are the soft young leaves and break off the stalk.
  2. Place the potatoes, green peas, and maize all at once in a pot. Ensure to cook the potatoes without chopping them up
  3. Add enough water just to cover the contents and place the pumpkin leaves on top and cover the lid.
  4. Cook over medium heat for approximately forty minutes. Please note that you should stir the food at this point.
  5. Afterwards, drain the excess water and add margarine or butter and salt to taste and an extra layer of flavor
